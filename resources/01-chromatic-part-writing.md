# SATB Part Writing 7: Chromatic Part Writing
Source: LearnMusicTheory.net

## Key Principles

1. Follow all guidelines and principles of diatonic part writing.
2. Resolve tendency tones FIRST (LTs, 7ths, b2 in N, +6 in +6 chords, V+, etc.).
3. **Chromatically raised notes usually resolve upward; lowered notes often resolve downward.**
4. Keep the chromatic tone in the same voice as the unaltered note to avoid cross-relations.

## Cross-Relations

A cross-relation occurs when a pitch in one voice is followed by a chromatic alteration of the same pitch in another voice (like G#-G♮). Cross-relations are best avoided in part-writing; they overemphasize the chromatic pitch.

### Common Cross-Relations to Avoid:

#### 1. Subtonic and leading tone in minor keys
- In minor: subtonic (♮7) vs leading tone (#7)
- Keep them in the same voice

#### 2. Tonicization of vi in major keys
- When tonicizing vi, the temporary leading tone (raised note) can resolve down a third in inner voice
- Example in C: G# → E when moving from V7/vi to vi

#### 3. N6 followed by V
- When Neapolitan 6 resolves to V, the cross relation between ♭2 and ♮2 is ALLOWED
- This allows ♭2 to move down to the leading tone

#### 4. +6 chords resolving to V7
- When augmented 6th chord resolves to V7, the raised note "SLIDES" down to avoid cross-relation
- The +6 becomes the 7th of V7
